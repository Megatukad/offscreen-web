FAQ

Before you ask.

Will this sound like me, or like every other AI account?

Like you. Before any content gets made, I build a voice guide from how you actually write, and your tools pull from it. Generic output is an input problem. The voice guide is the input that fixes it.

Can I just do this myself?

You can. It takes about 30 to 40 hours: researching how to build a persona, testing prompt structures, working out what makes AI content stop sounding generic, building a content system from scratch, and documenting all of it so it holds together when you are not watching. The work is the obstacle, not the tools. This is that work, already done and structured to run reliably.

How is this different from hiring a freelancer on Fiverr?

A freelancer hands you an output. This hands you a system. A logo is not a brand. A few posts are not a content system. After this, you can brief a VA, an AI tool, or anyone else to continue the brand without starting over. That is what documentation and structure give you that a one-off hire does not.

Do I have to be on camera or show my face?

No. That is the whole point. Your brand runs on a generated persona, or a stylized version of you if you prefer. You never film anything.

What does it cost to keep running after handoff?

This is separate from the one-time build price. After handoff, the only ongoing cost is the tools you choose to run it, nothing to me. I'll recommend a setup that fits your budget. For most people that runs between $35 and $300 a month, depending on which AI writing tool and image tool you pick. You can start at the low end and move up only if you want to.

What do I own at the end? Can I use it without you?

Everything lives in your accounts: your Notion workspace, your files, your prompts. I have no ongoing access after delivery unless you invite me back. A one-time fee means no subscriptions and no dependency. If I shut down tomorrow, your brand system keeps running.

What if I am not happy with it?

The voice guide and positioning are reviewed and approved before I build anything on top of them, so we agree on direction first. If a deliverable misses the brief we agreed at kickoff, I revise it until it matches. You have a 14-day revision window after handoff.

What if I am pre-revenue, is this the right time?

It depends, but not on revenue. If you know what your brand is about and who it's for, pre-revenue is fine. A brand system speeds up how fast people come to trust you, and that helps most before the sales are rolling. The build only struggles if you don't yet know what the brand is about or who you are talking to, because the content needs a direction to point at. If you are that early, it is worth waiting until that part is settled.

How long does it take?

14 days from kickoff to handoff. The timeline depends on you returning the intake and your feedback on time.
